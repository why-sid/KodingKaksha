Master Java Programming This Winter

About the Bootcamp

Embrace the winter with our 3-week immersive Java Bootcamp, designed specifically for students in classes 6–12. Whether you're stepping into coding for the first time or looking to sharpen your skills, our program offers the perfect blend of learning, creativity, and fun!

Why this bootcamp works:

  • Gradual Learning: Our carefully crafted 3-week structure spreads out key concepts over time, ensuring students absorb and practice each lesson at a comfortable pace.
  • Simplified Projects: Each project is designed to be achievable within the given timeframe, allowing students to reinforce their skills through hands-on experience and real-world applications.
  • Practical Focus: The final week is entirely dedicated to real-world projects, with a focus on building portfolio-worthy applications and honing presentation skills, preparing students for future success in coding and beyond.

Join us and experience a bootcamp that not only teaches Java programming but also equips you with practical problem-solving skills and confidence to take on future coding challenges!

Seats: Limited to 10 students only
Start Date: 3rd January, 2025
End Date: 24th January, 2025
Duration: 3 weeks

What You'll Learn

Week 1: Java Basics

Group A: Classes 6–8

  • Day 1: Introduction to Computers and Programming
  • Day 2: Java Basics – Variables, Data Types
  • Day 3: Input/Output in Java
  • Day 4: Conditional Statements (if/else)
  • Day 5: Loops (for, while)
  • Day 6: Simple Practice Problems (e.g., print patterns)
  • Day 7: Mini Project – Simple Calculator

Week 2: Intermediate Concepts

Group A: Classes 6–8

  • Day 1: Functions – Writing and Calling
  • Day 2: Arrays – Basics and Operations
  • Day 3: Simple Patterns with Loops
  • Day 4: Debugging Simple Errors
  • Day 5: Problem-Solving with Arrays
  • Day 6: Building Small Programs (e.g., Prime Numbers Checker)
  • Day 7: Mini Project – Guess the Word Game

Week 3: Final Projects and Presentation

Group A: Classes 6–8

  • Day 1: Introduction to Final Projects
  • Day 2: Building a Final Project (e.g., Number Guessing Game)
  • Day 3-5: Debugging and Improvement
  • Day 6: Preparing Presentation
  • Day 7: Final Presentation
